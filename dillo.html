Armadillo Habitats

Discover where these unique creatures live

Introduction

Armadillos are small to medium-sized mammals known for their armored shells. They are native to the Americas and live in a range of habitats.

Preferred Environments

  • Grasslands: Open areas with soft soil ideal for burrowing.
  • Forests: Especially deciduous forests with dense underbrush.
  • Wetlands: Some species are found near streams and swamps.
  • Deserts: Certain species are adapted to dry, arid conditions.

Burrowing Behavior

Armadillos are excellent diggers and use their claws to create underground burrows where they sleep, hide from predators, and raise their young.

Geographic Distribution

Most armadillo species are found in Central and South America, but the nine-banded armadillo has expanded its range into the southern United States.
